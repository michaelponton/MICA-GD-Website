Thank You Greensboro: Spring Break in Alabama

Contributed by Alexa Veneziano & Paige Bowers

November 12, 2015 — 12:33 PM EST

Ten design students headed down to Project M this past spring break for a Blitz, a mini intensive one-week project in Greensboro, Alabama, a small southern town just outside of Birmingham. Accompanied by Aleks Safavora ’10, Kailie Parrish ’11 and faculty member Ryan Clifford, students spent seven days exploring and experiencing all this town has to offer. Stuffing themselves with barbecue and sweet tea, visiting local sites and monuments and wandering and meeting people in the local shops and streets, the MICA students had an alternate and exciting spring break.


Close to the conclusion of their trip, they decided upon a design project to send their gratitude out to this beautifully welcoming town. With a letterpress at their service and multiple creative minds, the team created the world’s biggest thank you cards for the town; “Dear Greensboro, Thank you for”. The cards were signed and delivered to all the people they had met in the week’s passing. Personalized with hand lettering and inside jokes, the cards flooded the town shop windows and brought the biggest smiles to the town’s faces.


Project M is a program that gathers creative people who are already inspired to contribute to the greater good, and are looking for a platform to collaborate, generate ideas and projects bigger than themselves. Students are encouraged to create positive change, challenge the status quo, and most importantly, think wrong.


Paige and Alexa are sophomore design majors.
